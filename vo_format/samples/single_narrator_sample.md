# The Keeper's Letter

The lighthouse had stood dark for a year before the harbor board sent
anyone to clear it out. They found the letter under the lamp housing,
folded twice, dry despite everything. The keeper had written it in a steady
hand:

    "The light is out and I am going with it. Don't waste grief on the lamp.
    Waste it on the fact that no one came up here to trim the wick."

Below that was a column of tide times, and a single name, scratched out so
hard the nib had torn the page.
