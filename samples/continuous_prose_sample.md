# The Cartographer's Country

She drew coastlines for the admiralty, and she always drew them twice. The
first copy was for the sailors — every shoal and sounding they could steer
by. The second she kept for herself, and on that one she let the ink follow
what the surveys had only guessed at.

                    *

Corrections trickled in for eleven years. A reef in the wrong place. A
river mouth that had shifted a mile north. She fixed the sailors' copy each
time and never touched her own. When she died, both maps turned up rolled
in the same tube, and it was the private one, the guesswork one, that
matched the new soundings almost line for line. She had never once been to
sea.
